# Glossary & Terminology

**Admin Portal** — Internal portal used by MOE staff ("Admin users") to manage accounts, top-ups, course/fee configuration, and FAS scheme eligibility rules.

**e-Service Portal** — Public-facing portal used by Account Holders and students to view balances/transactions, update personal details, view course fees, pay fees, and apply for financial assistance.

**Account Holder (AH)** — The person who holds the Education Account and logs into the e-Service Portal. The AH is also a student in most cases.

**Education Account** — A dedicated financial account, topped up only by MOE, set aside to save or pay for education-related expenses. This is the **only** account terminology this platform uses for post-secondary fee deduction and FAS-linked payments.

> **Important terminology note:** Some external/government materials refer to a similar concept as **PSEA (Post-Secondary Education Account)**. On this platform, that concept is always called **"Education Account."** If a user asks about "PSEA," treat it as referring to the Education Account on this platform — do not describe separate PSEA rules, as this platform does not model PSEA as a distinct external scheme.

**Gross Household Income (GHI)** — Total combined monthly income of all household members (employment/trade income, regular allowances, employee CPF contributions, and other income sources such as pension or rental income). Excludes National Service allowance, severance/insurance payouts, and alimony for child/ex-spouse maintenance.

**Per Capita Income (PCI)** — GHI divided by the number of members in the same household. `PCI = Monthly GHI / household size`.

**FAS** — Financial Assistance Scheme(s) administered by MOE, covering different tiers/benefits depending on education level (JC/CI, Independent Schools, ITE/Polytechnic/University).

**Course fee** — Includes fee components such as course fee, miscellaneous fee, and GST, calculated monthly per student per course.

**Top-Up** — MOE-initiated credit to an Account Holder's Education Account, done on a regular or ad-hoc basis, in batches or to specific account holders, based on configurable rules (age, balance, schooling status, etc).
