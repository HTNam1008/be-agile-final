# FAQs

**Q: What is the duration of FAS?**
A: FAS is granted until December of the current year. Students need to re-apply to continue receiving FAS in the next academic year, as household income and family profile may change year to year.

**Q: Is there a cut-off date to apply?**
A: No. There is no cut-off date — applications can be submitted at any time.

**Q: What is included in household income for FAS calculations?**
A: For employed household members, income declared to the CPF Board (or assumed under CPF Regulations). For other income earners, it includes taxable rental, dividend, and investment income, averaged over 12 months based on the latest available IRAS income tax assessment.

**Q: Do I still have to pay fees in cash if I'm an FAS recipient?**
A: FAS covers both school fees and miscellaneous fees at the qualifying tier — refer to the relevant eligibility/benefits chunk (chunk-03 or chunk-04) for the specific level. Any remaining balance not covered should be confirmed against the student's specific scheme tier rather than assumed to be zero.

**Q: Can I appeal if I don't meet the income criteria?**
A: If the income criteria aren't met, the institution may consider the student for its own institution-based financial assistance scheme. Approach the institution directly — this is evaluated case-by-case.

**Q: If my child/I am transferring institutions, do I need a new application?**
A: If transferring to another Government or Government-aided institution during the year, FAS status generally continues, provided there are no changes to household income. Confirm with the receiving institution as a final check.

**Q: When does FAS take effect after I submit my application?**
A: Generally, if approved by the end of a given month (with accurate, complete information), FAS takes effect from the following month.
